About me

I am an enthusiastic oil and pastel painter, having a deep passion for art. I have been painting with oils for more than 10 years, mostly landscapes, flowers, seascapes and sometimes portraits.

I paint various subjects and scenes, focusing on the colors and especially on the mood of the scene. Although I discovered soft pastels a lot later, I especially love using them because of their vibrant colors and soft application on fine sanded papers.

Although using pastels is often considered drawing, I consider it painting because of its similar application of color and finished look. Through trial and error I have become very comfortable working with colors, developing my own style of painting, which is somewhere between impressionism and realism.

I wish to produce colourful paintings which make the admirer feel the passion and the positive energy of the painting. My biggest personal goal as a painter is to sell paintings to homes in which my painting is a part of their living area, being appreciated and admired every day.

Materials I use

I only use soft pastels from the best soft pastel companies, mostly from Schmincke, Sennelier, Unison, Blue Earth and Jackson's.

All of their pastels are incredibly pigmented and most importantly, do not fade over time. The pastels and the acid-free papers I use all ensure the paintings are suitable for galleries and will stay the same for hundreds of years.

Framing pastel paintings

Pastel paintings have to be framed in a specific way to ensure any dust from the pastels does not get on the passe-partout or stuck on the glass.

I create additional space between the painting and passe-partout with foam in between them. I also cover all the insides with double sided adhesive tape which ensures any pastel dust falling of the painting stays in on the tape behind the passe-partout.
